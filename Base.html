Facts About Earth

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life.
  • About 71% of Earth's surface is covered by water.
  • Earth has one natural satellite: the Moon.
  • The atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen and oxygen.

Global Warming

Global warming refers to the long-term rise in Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, primarily the emission of greenhouse gases from burning fossil fuels. Effects include rising sea levels, more extreme weather, and loss of biodiversity.

Timeline of Earth

  • 4.5 billion years ago: Earth forms.
  • 3.8 billion years ago: First life appears.
  • 540 million years ago: Cambrian explosion of life.
  • 65 million years ago: Dinosaurs go extinct.
  • 200,000 years ago: Modern humans emerge.
  • Present: Human-driven climate change.
